## A trading platform is a control system

Trading products are less about charts and more about feedback loops. Every UI element should make the state of the system legible: exposure, execution quality, and decision confidence.

## Build around risk first

If the system cannot explain risk, it should not be able to trade. Risk checks must run before execution, during execution, and after fills are received.

## Logging beats guessing

Good trading software creates a forensic trail. That means orders, fills, rejections, and user actions are all visible with clear timestamps and shared identifiers.

## Reliable systems feel calm

The highest-quality trading interfaces are quiet, precise, and predictable. Users should spend their attention on decisions, not on deciphering the platform.
