## Production starts at the edges

The first failure mode in AI products is rarely the model. It is the coupling between prompts, data access, orchestration, and unsupported assumptions. A production system should be boring at the boundaries and flexible in the middle.

## The architecture I trust

I separate the system into four layers: input normalization, retrieval and reasoning, execution, and evaluation. Each layer has a narrow contract. That makes it easier to test, safer to change, and much easier to replace later.

## What matters operationally

- Deterministic input validation
- Tracing on every tool call
- Fallbacks for empty or stale context
- Offline evaluations before release
- Human-readable failure states

## The real goal

The best AI products do not feel clever. They feel reliable. The user does not notice the architecture when it is right, but they immediately feel when it is missing.
